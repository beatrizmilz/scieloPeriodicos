About the journal

Basic Information

 

REAd publishes essays and theoretical-empirical works from different areas of Administration. We welcome works from different onto-epistemological strands, with high theoretical consistency and methodological rigor (when applicable). Although REAd includes contributions to the practice of Administration, it is a journal oriented to the academic community. Therefore, works that are restricted to the application of management models and technologies are not accepted. Contributions submitted for evaluation must be unpublished (in any language) and must not be under consideration in another journal. The text cannot contain any information about the authors, review comments or other form of identification of authorship in the submission and review rounds. Furthermore, it needs to be free from plagiarism or self-plagiarism.

Areas of interest:

REAd is open to all areas related to the management of organizations.

Background:

The Electronic Review of Administration (REAd) was launched in 1995, being published by the School of Management of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul, it is the first electronic publication in the field in Latin America. Over 600 published manuscripts and an average of 800 daily accesses of REAders in Brazil and abroad, REAd was granted A-level status as a journal of nationwide circulation by the CAPES Qualis System and other indexers as well. Recently it has been approved to enter the SciELO collection.

REAd’s whole collection of texts is available from the website www.REAd.ea.ufrgs.br. A periodic journal, having become triannual in 2007, REAd is open to all themes related to organizational management. Currently, the special editions in English are published online in the journal's website, at Editions. In March 2012, had an average of 839 visitors per week.

From July 2017 to August 2019, Maria Ceci Misoczky was the editor-in-chief of REAd.

Every 4 months

Instructions to authors

 

 

1. Scope and policy

REAd publishes essays and theoretical-empirical works from different areas of Administration. We welcome works from different onto-epistemological strands, with high theoretical consistency and methodological rigor (when applicable). Although REAd includes contributions to the practice of Administration, it is a journal oriented to the academic community. Therefore, works that are restricted to the application of management models and technologies are not accepted. Contributions submitted for evaluation must be unpublished (in any language) and must not be under consideration in another journal. The text cannot contain any information about the authors, review comments or other form of identification of authorship in the submission and review rounds. Furthermore, it needs to be free from plagiarism or self-plagiarism.

 

2. Peer Review Process

REAd's evaluation process is completely anonymous and peer reviewed. The evaluators are PhD professors from several institutions.

The works undergo a pre-evaluation by the editor (desk review) and, if accepted, are sent to the evaluators, anonymously (double blind review).

At this stage, the existence of one recommendation for rejection is considered enough for the editorial decision on rejecting the paper.

Submissions can be in Portuguese, Spanish or English. The complete work will be published in the language in which it is submitted.

The work must comply with the ABNT standards used by the journal and the parameters established in the Guidelines for Authors.

 

3. Open Access Policy

This journal provides immediate open access to its content on the principle that scientific knowledge freely available to the public provides greater democratization of knowledge worldwide. 

 

4. Privacy Statement

The names and addresses given in this magazine will be used exclusively for the services provided by this publication and will not be made available for other purposes or to third parties.

 

5. Publication ethics and malpractice statement

As members of a journal of national and international scope, we editor-in-chief, executive committee, associate editors, editorial board and executive team, are committed to the "Principles of Transparency and Good Practice in Academic Publications" from the COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) which aim to encourage the identification of plagiarism, bad practices, fraud, possible ethics violations and prosecution. For this reason, authors should visit the COPE website http://publicationethics.org, which contains information for authors and editors about research ethics.

We follow the Criteria, policy and procedures for the admission and permanence of scientific journals in the SciELO Brazil Collection.

Our publication ethics is in addition to the San Francisco Declaration on Research Assessment (DORA), to which we are signatories.

 

6. Erratum and Retraction

The procedures adopted in cases necessary to register and publish erratum and retractions are based on the standards presented by the SciELO Brazil Collection and observe the COPE Retraction Guidelines.

 

7. Copyright Notice

The author authorizes REAd to publish the article on its website or in printed editions, not implying the payment of copyright or any other fee to the authors, and certifies that this article has not been published, to date, in any Brazilian journal.

 

8. Archiving

This journal uses the LOCKSS system to create a distributed archival system among participating libraries and allows them to create permanent journal archives for preservation and restoration.

 

 

Manuscript preparation

 

Guidelines for the Publication of Scientific Manuscripts - REAd

1. General Rules:

As part of the submission process, authors are required to verify the submission's compliance with all of the items below. Submissions that do not comply with the rules will be rejected and the authors informed.

To be an original and unpublished contribution, compatible with the Journal's Editorial Line.

Not being in the process of evaluation in another national or international publication.

Be signed by a maximum of 4 (four) authors.

The manuscript file cannot contain any form of identification of the authors, including review comments. The “Author's Properties” need to be cleared. Author and text metadata must be included in the online submission form.

In addition to this file, another page will be submitted containing the title, name and information about each author or author (Orcid, Institution of work/study, activity carried out, research or activism themes, address for postal and electronic correspondence).

Along with the article, the author must send a Declaration of Originality.

 

2. Formatting:

Font Times New Roman 12; 1.5 cm line spacing with justified paragraphs; A4 size sheets, with 2 cm bottom and right margins and 3 cm left and top margins; no page numbering. Title, abstract (maximum 200 words), 3 to 5 keywords (separated by point) in the three languages in which the Journal accepts submissions (Spanish, English and Portuguese), being presented first in the language in which the manuscript is written. Maximum: 30 pages.

The notes must be in footnotes and with continuous numbering.

Figures must be inserted in the body of the text and also included in separate files, in JPG, in the required resolutions (see below). Other aspects not informed, use NBR 6023 for illustrations.

The tables follow the recommendations of the IBGE/ABNT Foundation and must be inserted in the text and opened for editing. They do not need to be included in separate files, just inserted in the text, open for editing.

The resolution of the illustrations must be as follows: 300dpi for images (photos and the like); 900dpi for drawings (charts, graphs, schematics or other type of illustration done in drawing tool).

As stated above, the texts can be submitted in Portuguese, Spanish or English. The title, abstract and keywords must be presented in the three languages. The works must be submitted rigorously revised, according to the grammatical norms in force in the chosen language.

The textual transcripts of authors that do not exceed five lines will be kept in the main body of the text, in quotation marks and without italics or bold. If the transcription has more than five lines, it will be presented separately from the main body of the text, with a space before and another one after, with a 4 cm indent from the margin, without using quotation marks, italics or bold.

Quotations and references must comply with ABNT standards.

All “URL” addresses must be active.

The text must be free of plagiarism or self-plagiarism.

Upon submission, the article will be submitted to a Desk Review, and may be rejected in this procedure or sent to the evaluation process.

Cases not provided for in these rules will be resolved by REAd's Editors.

 

3. Assessment criteria:

  • Topic (current and relevant)
  • Aims (clear and well defined)
  • Literature review (reflects the state-of-the-art in the field of knowledge)
  • Conceptual or theoretical consistency of the work (adequate and well-structured)
  • Research method used (clearly defined and consistent with the objectives of the work)
  • Data analysis and results (correct interpretation of the data and combined with the theoretical basis)
  • Conclusions (clear, objective and supported by the research data)
  • Contribution (towards the particular field of knowledge)
  • Writing and organization of the text (spelling, grammar, clarity, objectivity and formal structure)

 

4. Articles, reviews and interviews

4.1. Articles – essays or theoretical-empirical papers

The manuscript should be scientific and present research results that are relevant or contribute to knowledge in the field of Management.

The manuscript must be unpublished, that is, it must not have been submitted for evaluation or published in another journal. Note: We accept papers presented at scientific congresses and published in proceedings. The author can only submit one article per year.

Every paper or case submitted must be sent in accordance with the REAd rules, accompanied by the completed Author Registration Form + Declaration of Originality signed by the first author. The files should be named according to their content.

* For manuscripts with more than one author, each author must fill out a Form with his/her details, starting that the order of authorship in the manuscript.

* The Author Registration Forms should come attached to the email, along with the manuscript to be submitted, even if the author has registered previously.

Please note! The text of the manuscript should be checked prior to submission in all the required languages and conform to the standards of the journal and the ABNT (NBR 6022, 2003). Submissions that do not conform to these standards m be rejected.

In the event that the journal requests the files be RESENT, it is important to send all the files again, and reply via the same email, and MAINTAIN the history of the previous messages. The files should be named according to their content. Example: article, case, record, abstracts, declaration, etc.

The manuscript should be in a file from Word 97-2003, without notes or markings that reveal authorship, and should be less than 1MB.

Some examples of manuscript presentation and other documents can be found in the Forms section, in the journal’s website.

The general parameters are for consultation and can be followed in the case that there are no specifications in the journal’s standards regarding some aspect of the text.

Manuscripts should follow the ABNT standards (NBR 6022, 2003) for scientific manuscripts, except in the formats specified below.

4.2. Reviews

Reviews can be proposed for the following types of documents:

  • books – classics or recent publications, which must not exceed 8 pages; if the review article refers to a set of works articulated by a common theme, it may reach a maximum of 20 pages.
  • films – as long as the connection with the theme of the magazine is clear and that it encourages reflection, without exceeding 8 pages; if the review article refers to a set of films articulated by a common theme, it may reach a maximum of 20 pages.
  • websites and other internet formats - covering everything from critical analysis to information about relevant exchanges on social media, it must not exceed 15 pages.
  • events – from academic events to militant actions in the streets, preferably original reports but in cases of particular relevance will also be considered reflective reviews of media coverage, should not exceed 20 pages.

4.3. Interviews

This section accepts writings that explore themes in a dialogic format, less structured than the previous ones. Allows the examination of themes and ideas from alternative political and epistemological positions or still in production. There is no page limit. However, the Editor reserves the right to suggest revisions in case of excessively long material.

 

5. Permanent call for papers

Theme: Administration's contributions to face the crisis in times of pandemic

REAd – Revista Eletrônica de Administração wishes to contribute to the process of reflection and production of knowledge about these extraordinary times that we must live. Therefore, it invites the submission of papers, in continuous flow, on the crisis related to the new coronavirus pandemic (SARS-CoV-2).

The many faces of the crisis and the strategies to face it, in all affected countries, have evident relationships with the field of Administration, as can easily be seen in topics such as: (re)organization of health systems; directing production processes to provide essential equipment; emergency training and reorganization of work teams; transformations in the work process; scientific and technological development with an emphasis on the medical-pharmaceutical-industrial complex; uses of technology to control population groups; socioeconomic impacts on societies alREAdy marked by deep inequalities; public policy; tensions in the federative pact; solidarity networks and the strengthening of the social economy; contributions from universities and research centers.

The thematic possibilities mentioned above are only indicative, since the complex dimensions of this social phenomenon are expressed in multiple ways in the disciplinary space of Administration and in the indispensable interdisciplinary dialogue.

The works that respond to this call will be published in preprint, in accordance with the SciELO platform knowledge access policy. All contributions must be consistent with the focus and scope of the REAd and observe the guidelines for authors.

 

6. Submissions

Submissions must be made exclusively through the Electronic Journal Publishing System, SEER, available at http://seer.ufrgs.br/index.php/REAd/index. The journal does not accept submissions that do not observe the following rules. There is no fee for submission and evaluation of articles.

 

6.1 Submission Preparation Checklist

As part of the submission process, authors are required to check off their submission's compliance with all of the following items, and submissions may be returned to authors that do not adhere to these guidelines.
