About the journal

 

Basic information

 

Nova Economia is a scholarly journal edited by the Economics Department, Federal University of Minas Gerais (UFMG), Brazil. Created in 1990, the journal publishes original research papers and reviews in economics and related discipinary fields, while adopting a pluralistic editorial stance, welcoming papers in all research traditions and theoretical approaches.

The jornal is published three times a year. Its abbreviated title is Nova econ., which should be used in bibliographies, footnotes and bibliographical references and strips.

Instructions to authors

 

Aims and scope

 

Nova Economia is edited by the Department of Economic Sciences of the Federal University of Minas Gerais. Founded in 1990, it publishes articles and reviews on theoretical and applied economics and related areas. The journal adopts a pluralistic orientation, opening itself up to different currents of economic thought and research orientations.

Editorial deliberations are guided exclusively by criteria of academic quality. Submitted works are evaluated by external reviewers, preserving the anonymity of authors and reviewers during the evaluation process (double-blind review).

Nova Economia does not charge any fee for submission, evaluation, processing and publication of articles.

Nova Economia uses the iThenticate/CrossRef system to identify duplicate content in all submissions received.

The authors retain the copyright, only granting the journal the right of first publication.

 

 

Format and preparation of manuscripts

 

Rules for submission of papers:

1. Nova Economia receives and orders articles and review articles and publishes them in the order of their approval by members of the Editorial Board or by external reviewers. Articles in English approved for publication will be included in the next issue that is still in the initial phase of editorial preparation.

2. Manuscripts submitted for publication in the journal should not normally exceed 10,000 words in length (including references);

3. An abstract of no more than 150 words and a list of key words should accompany the paper;

4. Authors who had an article accepted for publication in the journal should wait at least twelve months before starting a new submission;

5. The submission of a paper to the journal implies that it has not been published and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. By submitting a manuscript, the authors agree that if it is accepted for publication, the exclusive copyright of the article will be assigned to Nova Economia;

6. References only those effectively cited in the body of the work - should be presented at the end of the text, in alphabetical order, in accordance with ABNT's NBR-6023 standard.

 

 

Submitting a manuscript

 

Authors must register on the journal's electronic management system in order to submit their manuscripts and follow the steps of the editorial process: <http://revistas.face.ufmg.br/novaeconomia>.

Registration in the system and subsequent access, through login and password, are mandatory for the submission of works, as well as to monitor the ongoing editorial process.
