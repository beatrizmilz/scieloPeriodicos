About the journal

 

Basic Information

 

The Revista Ciência Agronômica has been published by the Centro de Ciências Agrárias of the Federal University of Ceará since 1971. The journal was conceived as the natural result of the need to disseminate scientific knowledge in the area of the agricultural sciences developed by researchers from the then Escola de Agronomia. The articles published in the Revista Ciência Agronômica are available in their entirety starting with the first issue, published in 1971.

The target audience of the journal are the professionals, professors, researchers and students of higher education in the areas of the Agricultural Sciences, Food Engineering, Fisheries, Animal Science and Natural Resources.

The abbreviated title of the journal is Rev. Science Agron., which should be used in bibliographies, footnotes, references and bibliographic elements.

Articles should be submitted through the online system of the journal-OJS, at www.ccarevista.ufc.br. When submitting works for publication, authors should follow the Instructions to Authors.

The Revista Ciência Agronômica has been published exclusively in an electronic version from 2019, the printed version is no longer available.

Instructions to authors

 

Scope and Editorial Policy

 

Policy of Privacy  

The articles published by the Revista Ciência Agronômica are publicly accessible in their entirety, based on the principle of free dissemination of research results, thereby generating a greater exchange of knowledge. This policy leads to dissemination, wider reading, and citation of an author's scientific article.

All names, postal addresses and electronic addresses are strictly for the use of the Revista Ciência Agronômca and are not to be used for other purposes.

Copyright

When an article is approved, the authors grant an exclusive license to publish the submitted article in electronic format, including photographs, tables and figures that may be selected as the cover image.

Copyright law also requires authors to provide the Revista Ciência Agronômca with permission to use copyrighted material that has been published by other journals or publishers, which include figures, tables, and images. Such permission should be loaded into the system as a supplemental file.

Peer Review Process

Works approved preliminarily by the Editorial Committee will be sent to the section editors who will submit the manuscript to at least two reviewers in the area. Articles will be published based on originality, quality and scientific merit, with the editorial committee having the final decision. The identity of the authors and reviewers will remain confidential throughout the process. The journal administration will ensure that the reviewers of each article are, necessarily, from institutions other than that of the authors.

 

 

Form and preparation of manuscripts

 

Attention: The norms are subject to change, so be sure to consult them before submitting an article. The same are valid for all works submitted to the Revista Ciência Agronômca. An example of an article can be seen under ‘MODEL ARTICLE’ at http://www.ccarevista.ufc.br.

1. Editorial Policy

The Revista Ciência Agronômica is intended for the publication of original scientific and technical articles that have not been published by or submitted to any other journal in the areas of the Agricultural Sciences or Natural Resources.

Articles may be submitted to the Revista Ciência Agronômica in Portuguese, English or Spanish. If approved, the article should be translated into English for publication. The RCA requires that the translation be done by a specialised company. Some preferred companies are suggested below:

The cost of the translation into English will be borne for by the authors; proof of payment should be sent to the RCA when submitting the article, using the “Transfer of Supplementary Documents” field on the website.

Works submitted to the RCA will be preliminarily evaluated by the Editorial Committee and only then will be sent to at least two (02) reviewers in the area, and only published if approved by both reviewers and the editorial board. Articles will be published based on originality, quality and scientific merit, with the editorial committee having the final decision. The identity of the authors and reviewers will remain confidential throughout the process. The journal administration will ensure that the reviewers of each article are, necessarily, from institutions other than that of the authors. Any article with more than five authors will not be accepted by the Revista Ciência Agronômca, except under special conditions (see Authors). No a posteriori changes to the names of the authors will be allowed.

Publication fee: BRL60.00 (sixty Brazilian Reals) per formatted page

RCA no longer provides offprints or copies for authors. For on-line submission the following are required:

1. Agreement with the declaration of Copyright Responsibility, which should be signed by the respective authors and sent via the “Transfer Supplementary Documents” field;

2. All the authors should be registered in the system;

3. Only the final version of the article should contain the name of all the authors, identified in a footnote, including title;

4. Identification and full address of the corresponding author.

3. Formatting the Article

Typing: A maximum of 20 double-spaced pages in Times New Roman, normal, size 12 font with a paragraph indentation of 1 cm. All margins should be 2.5 cm. Lines should be numbered continuously.

Structure: Scientific articles should have the following order: title, title in English, authors, resumo in Portuguese, palavras-chave in Portuguese, abstract in English, keywords in English, introduction, material and methods, results and discussion, conclusions, acknowledgments (optional) and references. Scientific notes do not need to follow the structure of articles, but should contain the title in English, resumo and palavras-chave in Portuguese, and abstract and keywords in English.

Title: The title should be written with only the first letter capitalised, in bold type, centred on the page, and contain a maximum of 15 words. A numbered footnote, called from the title, should contain information on the nature of the work (whether extracted from a thesis/dissertation, whether funded research), including references to any collaborating institutions. The titles of the remaining sections of the structure (resumo, palavras-chave, abstract, keywords, etc) should be written with only the first letter capitalised, in bold type and left-justified.

Authors: The full names of the authors (with no abbreviations) should come below the title with only the first letter capitalised, one after the other, separated by a comma and centred on the line. A footnote on the first page should show the complete affiliation (department, centre, institution, city, state and country) and electronic address of each author; the full address of the corresponding author should also be shown. The corresponding author should be identified by an "*". Articles with more than five authors will only be accepted when the research can be shown to have been conducted in separate regions. In the first version of the submitted article, the names of the authors and the footnote should be omitted. The model to be adopted when inserting the names of the authors and footnotes in the final version of the article should follow that shown under ‘MODEL ARTICLE’ at www.ccarevista.ufc.br.

Resumo and Abstract: The Resumo and Abstract should begin with these words in the left margin, in bold type, with only the first letter capitalised; they should contain a maximum of 250 words.

Palavras-chave and Keywords: These should contain between three and five terms for indexing, which should not appear in the title. Each keyword should start with an uppercase letter and be followed by a period or full stop.

Introduction: The introduction should be compact and objective, containing current citations that are related to the subject addressed in the research. Any citations included in the introduction should be used to support discussion of the results, thereby setting the scene for a study of the method and discussion of the results. The introduction should contain no more than 550 words.

Citing authors in the text: Citations should follow the norms shown in ABNT No 10520 of August 2002.
Ex: Santos (2002) or (SANTOS, 2002); for two authors, use Pereira and Freitas (2002) or (PEREIRA; FREITAS, 2002); for three or more authors, use Xavier et al. (1997) or (XAVIER et al., 1997).

Tables: Tables should be numbered consecutively at the top using Arabic numerals. Vertical lines should not be used. Horizontal lines should be used to separate the title from the header and this from the content, plus an additional line at the end of the table. Each datum should occupy a different cell. Double-spacing should be used. Do not use bold or uppercase letters in the header. It is recommended that tables be 8.2 cm in width and not exceed 17 cm. See the table shown under ‘MODEL ARTICLE’ (www.ccarevista.ufc.br).

Figures: Graphs, photographs or drawings should have the general name of Figure followed by consecutive Arabic numerals; the legend should appear at the bottom. Graphs should be prepared using software compatible with Microsoft Windows. These should have a RESOLUTION of at least 500 dpi and be sent in separate files from the text file. Figures should be 8.2 cm in width and not exceed 17 cm. The font should be Times New Roman, size 10; bold type should not be used to identify the axes. The Revista Ciência Agronomica reserves the right not to accept tables and/or figures in landscape format, or which are more than 17 cm in width. Tables and Figures should be inserted immediately after they are first referenced.

Equations: Equations should be typed using the equation editor in Word, in Times New Roman font, and should be numbered consecutively using Arabic numerals.

The default sizes being:

Normal = 12 pt
Subscript/superscript = 8 pt
Sub-Subscript/superscript = 5 pt
Symbol = 18 pt
Sub-symbol = 14 pt

Statistics:

1. If an analysis of variance has been carried out, the value of ‘F’ and its significance should be shown;
2. Quantitative data should be worked using regression analysis;
3. The significance of the parameters of the regression equation should be shown;
4. Depending on the study (e.g. production function), the signs associated with the parameters should be analysed.
5. A minimum of four points are required to fit the regression equations.
6. The coefficients of the regression model should have the following format: y = a + bx + cx2 +...
7. The degree of freedom of the residual should be greater than 12.

Acknowledgments: Acknowledgements directed at individuals or institutions should come immediately after the conclusions, and should be serious and unambiguous, and show the reasons for their inclusion.

References: References should be prepared as per ABNT No 6023/2002. They should begin with the word REFERENCES (in uppercase, bold type and centred on the page), and should be typed in 12 font, double-spaced and justified. SIXTY PERCENT OF THE TOTAL REFERENCES SHOULD COME FROM INDEXED SCIENTIFIC JOURNALS WITH A PUBLICATION DATE OF LESS THAN 10 YEARS. References to books are not included in the above 60%. Citations of Abstracts, Annals, Technical Communications, Monographs, Dissertations and Theses will not be accepted in the references. For periodicals, information on the place of publication is not required, but the titles should not be abbreviated. A total of 20 to 30 references is recommended.

Some examples:

Book
NEWMANN, A. L.; SNAPP, R. R. Beef catlle. 7. ed. New York: John Willey, 1977. 883 p.

Chapter of a book
MALAVOLTA, E.; DANTAS, J. P. Nutrição e adubação do milho. In: PATERNIANI, E.; VIEGAS, G. P.Melhoramento e produção do milho. 2. ed. Campinas: Fundação Cargil, 1987. cap. 13, p.539-593.

Journal article
XAVIER, D. F.; CARVALHO, M. M.; BOTREL, M. A. Resposta de Cratylia argentea à aplicação em um solo ácido. Revista Brasileira de Zootecnia, v. 27, n. 01, p. 14-18, 1997.

ANDRADE, E. M. et al. (mais de 3 autores) Mapa de vulnerabilidade da bacia do Acaraú, Ceará, à qualidade das águas de irrigação, pelo emprego do GIS. Revista Ciência Agronômica, v. 37, n. 03, p. 280-287, 2006.

 

Thesis and dissertation

Theses and dissertations should not be cited.

Abstracts published at conferences or other events should not be cited.

  • All manuscripts should follow the criteria established by the International Codes of their respective area.
  • The opinions and concepts expressed in the articles are the exclusive responsibility of the authors.
  • Each manuscript submitted to the journal will be subject to screening for plagiarism and self-plagiarism.

 

3. Checklist - Revista Ciência Agronômca

Aiming at greater agility in the submission of articles, the Editorial Committee of the Revista Ciência Agronomica has prepared a checklist so that authors can check the format of their manuscript BEFORE submitting it for publication. The list was prepared in accordance with the norms of the Revista Ciência Agronômca. NEGATIVE answers mean that your article requires further adaptation to the norms of the journal. The submission of such articles will result in their return and a delay in processing.

POSITIVE answers mean that your article is in accordance with the norms and will be processed more quickly.

A. Regarding the work

1. Is the work original?
2. Does the work represent a scientific contribution to the field of Agricultural Sciences?
3. Is the work being submitted exclusively to the Revista Ciência Agronômca?

B. Regarding formatting

4. For work ready to be submitted online, are the names of the authors included?
5. Does the work contain a maximum of 20 pages? Is it in A4 format; is it double-spaced, including references; is the font Times New Roman, 12 pt, including titles and subtitles?
6. Are the margins set at 2.5 cm, the page numbers placed in the upper right margin, and are the lines numbered continuously? 
Was the paragraph indentation of 1 cm defined in the paragraph format? Remember that the journal does not accept paragraph indentation using the ‘TAB’ key or space bar.
8. Is the structure of the work in accordance with the norms of the journal, i.e. is it in the following order: title in Portuguese, title in English, authors, resumo in Portuguese, palavras-chave in Portuguese, abstract in English, keywords in English, introduction, material and methods, results and discussion, conclusions, acknowledgements (optional) and references?
9. Does the title have a maximum of 15 words?
10. Does the resumo in Portuguese and the abstract in English each have a maximum of 250 words?
11. Do the keywords contain between three and five terms, start with an uppercase letter and end with a period or full stop?
12. Does the introduction include citations that are current and related to the subject addressed in the research, and does it have a maximum of 550 words?
13. Were any citations included in the introduction used to support a discussion of the results?
14. Are the citations in accordance with the norms of the journal? 
15. Are the tables and figures formatted according to the norms of the journal, and are they inserted immediately after first being referenced? Remember, the use of ‘enter’ in cells that make up the tables is not permitted.
16. Are the tables, if any, in portrait format?
17. Are the figures of high quality (resolution of at least 500 dpi)?
18. Are the units and symbols used in your work within the norms of the International System adopted by the Revista Ciência Agronômica?
19. Are numbers separated by commas? Are the units separated from the number by a space? Remember, there should be no space between a number and the % symbol.
20. Does your work include between 20 and 30 references, 60% of which were published less than 10 years ago in indexed periodicals?
21. Is each reference cited in the text? 
22. Are all the references cited in the text correctly described according to the norms of the journal, and are they listed? 

C. Observations:

1. Remember that IF the rules of the journal are not strictly followed, your work will not be processed. It is therefore better to delay submitting your article for a few more days and check all the norms. Referring to work already published in your area may help to resolve any doubts and can act as a model (access the journal at http://www.ccarevista.ufc.br/busca).
2. If your responses are all AFFIRMATIVE, your work can be submitted with greater confidence. If you still have any NEGATIVE responses, your work will be returned, delaying the process.
Remember: If your article is returned a second time due to a problem with the norms, in particular the references, the submission will be cancelled, and the submission fee will not be refunded. It is therefore extremely important that the authors carefully check whether the article meets the norms required by the Revista Ciência Agronômica.
3. ALWAYS try to follow the status of your work in the online system of article management on the website of the journal (http://ccarevista.ufc.br).
4. This checklist does not replace the technical review made by the journal, to which all articles will be submitted.
