Sobre o Periódico

Basic Information

 

Full title: PERSPECTIVA TEOLÓGICA

Goals
- To disseminate theological reflection (in the systematic, biblical and pastoral scope) with recognized scientific quality, publishing original works that contribute to advancement in the area.
- To be instrumental in the service of constantly updating theology and clarifying the Christian faith in the face of issues of the post-modern world;
- To offer help, to those dedicated to theology, in their reflection and theological debate;
- To provide support to its readers to sustain, over time, the reason for their faith and hope (1Pt 3, 15).

Areas of interest
1) Theology of Christian Praxis (Christian Spirituality and Cultural and Religious Pluralism; Current Ethical Trends).

2) Systematic Theology (Biblical Sources of the Christian Tradition; Interpretation of the Christian Tradition in the Current Context).

History

The journal is published by the Graduate Theology Department of the Jesuit Faculty of Philosophy and Theology (PPGT-FAJE, Belo Horizonte). The printed version (ISSN 0102-4469) has been published regularly and uninterruptedly since 1969, existing, therefore, for 51 years. Since 2008, the journal has also been published in electronic form (ISSN 21768757), and all of its issues are fully available. “The didactic character of the fifty-year history of the Perspectiva Teológica can be divided into 3 phases. The first phase dates from its creation in 1969, when the College of Theology Cristo Rei, of São Leopoldo (RS), completed its twentieth anniversary as a college and joined the then newly-created University of Vale do Rio dos Sinos. The second phase began in 1982, when the College of Theology was installed in Belo Horizonte”. In that year, it received its new cover (2nd). The third phase gained new impetus in the 1990s, with the uninterrupted search for quality, listing in databases, bibliographies, directories, indexes, and with the improvement of technical issues (3rd cover). (Cf.: SUREKI, L. C. “Theological Perspective”: 50 years of theological reflection and ecclesial commitment. Perspectiva Teológica, Belo Horizonte, v. 51, n. 3, p. 15-43, Sep./Dec. 2019. Supplement.

In 2016, the DOI (Digital Object Identifier) was implemented. In mid-2017, all the research content was made available on the website in Portuguese, English and Spanish and the journal received its 4th cover “which is an external expression of internal changes. These concern the firm decision of the editorial team and of the professors of the Department of Theology to consolidate the steps taken, observing strictly the demanding process for quality and adaptation of the journal in order to implement the Capes requirements of periodicals; to seek listing in the reference platforms of international scientific citations and, thus, become a renowned journal in the field” (SUREKI, 2019, p. 19). It was classified in the QUALIS A1 stratum and entered into the EMERGING, DIADORIM, DOAJ collections.

In 2018, it achieved success in its national and international indexes. It was indexed in the WEB OF SCIENCE - Arts & Humanities database, in the Open Academic Journals Index - OAJI.NET; in the Free Portal, in the OASISBR Portal, Brazilian Portal of Scientific Periodicals in open access, of the Brazilian Institute of Information in Science and Technology - IBICT). It was listed in the SUNCAT Collective Catalog, in the Global Resources Network online research catalog, and in Scopus, the largest citation database in the world. Also, in 2018, the articles started to adopt the ORCID (Connecting Research and Researchers). According to bibliometric and sociometric studies of scientific production from 1969 to 2019/1, the journal "is the first scientific journal linked to a Graduate Program in Theology of Catholic confession in Brazil, and the second oldest in the country". See: VILLAS BOAS, A.; SERRATO, A. C.; ZDZIARSKI, A. D.; CRUZ, J. A. W. Perspectiva Teológica: memory, identity and frontiers. Bibliometric and sociometric study of scientific production from 1969 to 2019.1. p. 73. Available at: http://www.faje.edu.br/periodicos/index.php/perspectiva/article/view/4246/4308>. 
Perspectiva Teológica has exchanges with 194 journals (Africa, North America, Central America, South America, Asia and Europe).

Frequency: quarterly (January / April; May / August; September / December)

Instruções aos autores2

Scope and Policy

 

Perspectiva Teológica is classified as A1 in the QUALIS system (in the area of Science of Religion and Theology). Its main objective is to disseminate theological reflection with scientific quality that contributes significantly to advancement in the area. It publishes thematic articles, general articles, reviews and bibliographic notes. Texts may be sent in Portuguese, Spanish, English, French and Italian. Articles should be unpublished and not be released or under consideration in other journals. Authorship and coauthorship (if any) is reserved only for holders of doctoral degrees. In coauthored articles, the individual authors must assume responsibility for the part which corresponds to them, in the work. If the manuscript is approved, the authors should indicate what their personal contributions were, at the end of the text, after the list of bibliographic references. The authors are “responsible for ensuring the veracity of the information (data, content and conclusions) contained in the articles, and for ensuring the originality and priority of publication in the Journal”, as well as other obligations listed under the topic Responsibilities of the authors, available at:
<http://faje.edu.br/periodicos/index.php/perspectiva/about/#custom-0>.

Studies published in the areas of interest mentioned in the item “Basic Information”, in this form, dialogue with other fields of scientific knowledge that contribute to the service of faith and the commitment to justice. The journal is intended for researchers in the areas mentioned above, the academic public (undergraduate, graduate), pastoral agents and other interested persons. The editorial board, listed below, is composed of professors of PPGT-FAJE, of renowned researchers from Brazil and abroad, from different religious denominations and from areas of knowledge conversant with Theology.

Perspectiva Teológica adopts the double-blind evaluation system. The editors strive to ensure that peer review occurs transparently, and without infringing on the rights of both parties. The primary function of the reviewers is “to support the Editorial Committee in decisions relative to the articles submitted. Arbitrating on the scientific quality of the texts, issuing opinions that are reasoned, clear and objective; verifying the appropriateness of the theme as to the norms and mission of the journal, indicating reformulations when necessary to improve the texts. The focus of the opinion takes into consideration: the theoretical-critical and methodological dimensions of the text, and the contribution of the manuscript to the advancement of knowledge. For more information on the role of the reviewers, refer to the topic, Responsibilities of ad hoc reviewers in the Declaration is ethics and conduct in publication, available at <http://faje.edu.br/periodicos/index.php/perspectiva/about/#custom-0>.

Additional information

Privacy Policy:  the names and addresses informed in this journal will be used exclusively for the services provided by this publication, and will not be made available for other purposes or to third parties. Signed materials, declarations, opinions and concepts expressed in the articles are the responsibility of the authors.

Financing: Authors who receive financial support for research and whose articles have been approved for publication, should duly acknowledge the financial assistance after the bibliographic references.

Archiving: This journal utilizes the LOCKSS system to create a file system distributed among participating libraries, allowing them to create permanent archives of the journal for preservation and restoration.

Form and preparation of manuscripts

 

The form and preparation of manuscripts for publication is as follows: 1) the evaluation report is sent to the author; 2) the author revises the text and sends it to the editorial staff; 3) the assistant editor checks the revision done by the author; 4) texts are forwarded for revision: abstract, language in which it was written and bibliographic references, work conducted by professors of the Graduate Program in Theology (PPGT) of the Jesuit Faculty of Philosophy and Theology (FAJE), and editorial interns; 5) formating; 6) PPGT professors review the journal; 7) designer does the final finishing following the revisions; 8) the issue is sent in PDF format to the print shop (Gráfica FORMAT: <http://graficaformato.com.br/>; 9) PPGT teachers conduct a final review of the “pilot”; 10) the final review is approved; 11) online and print publication of the journal.

Perspectiva Teológicadoes not charge APC.

Manuscript evaluation policy

 

Peer Review Process
The process of evaluating articles sent to the Perspectiva Teológica is as follows: 1) analysis of the adequacy criteria and standards for publication in the Journal Perspectiva Teológica. Papers are rejected for the following reasons: a) the author and/or co-author does not have a doctor’s degree, prerequisite of a QUALIS A1 journal (except for book reviews which can be written by doctoral students and doctors); b) the standard format of a scientific paper: abstract, keywords, introduction, conclusion, references. 2) Preliminary evaluation by the Editorial Board. At this phase, plagiarism detection programs are used for article control (iThenticate Plagiarism Detection, AntiPlagiarist – ACNP; CopySpider) and the assessment of the relevance and methodological and scientific quality of the text by the professors of the Post-Graduation program in Theology (PPGT) of the Jesuit Faculty of Philosophy and Theology (FAJE). 3) Double blind peer review. The articles are sent without any identification of the author, to two ad hoc reviewers of the thematic area. The LATTES platform was used to select the reviewers and the thematic areas. The reviewer’s opinion will take into consideration the theoretical-critical and methodological dimensions of the text, as well as the contribution of the production, innovation and diffusion of knowledge. The evaluation should be concluded in 20 days. Conflicts between evaluative positions will be resolved by other reviewers and / or members of the Editorial Board. The final decision on any conflict and on the publication of the manuscript is reserved for the Editorial Commission (Director, Editor, Deputy Editor) that will take into account the entire evaluation process and the code of conduct for editorial standards. 4) The conclusion of the evaluation process: the article, once accepted for publication and approved by the author, is then prepared for printing. After corrections, the manuscript will be resent to author for the final verification. The entire process between the submission of the article and the final approval could prolong up to five full months.

In case authors withdraw from the evaluation process after their article has been accepted, with or without the due corrections, and if, after some time, they decide to resubmit their texts, these will be reassessed.

Conflict of interests

 

Facing the conflicts between evaluative positions (by authors and reviewers), the article may be sent to other evaluators and/or members of the Editorial Commission. The final decision on any conflicts and on the publication of the text is reserved for the Editorial Committee (Director, Editor, Assistant Editor) who will take into account the combined evaluative process and the code of conduct for editorial norms.

Plagiarism and Editorial Misconduct

 

Plagiarism: the application for verification of plagiarism used by the journal, which is protected by international copyright and trademark registration laws, is the iThenticate Plagiarism Detection Software.

Publication of Errata: as instructed by SciELO, when errors are observed in the article or other published document, that do not invalidate them, the Editor will make an Errata informing the corrections and/or additions to the document. The online version of the periodical will show the date of correction and the link to the print errata. The text must contain: the DOI document number that has error and other information, at the discretion of the Editor, URL link of the article.

Retraction: on identifying a problem in an article published in the journal, based on the SciELO model, it is necessary to proceed with the registration and publication of retraction. The text to be corrected should be presented by those responsible, along with the justifications, containing basic data (title, author, affiliation and abstract). It will not be "deleted from the vehicle where it was originally published", however, it will appear in the abstract "[Article Reviewed]". The PDF of the article will have watermark bars with the expression "Article Reviewed". This process is followed by the editorial team, the Editorial Board and the validation of some members of the Editorial Board.

The process of identifying misconduct, plagiarism, errata, retraction, check the text available at: <http://faje.edu.br/periodicos/index.php/perspectiva/about/#custom-0>.
